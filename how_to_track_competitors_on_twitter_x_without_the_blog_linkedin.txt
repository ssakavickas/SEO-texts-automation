Most teams think they need Twitter's official API to track competitors. They don't.

The Pro API costs $5,000 per month. The signals you actually need cost a fraction of that — or nothing at all.

Here is what competitive Twitter intelligence actually looks like in practice:

- Content patterns reveal what is working in your market before you waste budget testing it yourself
- Follower velocity and recent followers show which audiences competitors are winning right now
- Real-time monitoring catches product launches and strategic moves 10-15 days before quarterly reports surface them

The method you choose depends on your team, not your ambition. Free tools like TweetDeck handle casual monitoring. Scraping APIs and stream webhooks handle automated, scalable tracking. No-code tools like n8n bridge the gap for non-technical teams.
