The Twitter API used to be a straightforward decision. Then X killed the free tier, moved real access to $5,000/month, and forced most builders to rethink from scratch.

Here is what that actually means in practice: a basic monitoring bot checking three keywords every 15 minutes will burn through the $100/month Basic plan's entire monthly tweet allocation in under two days. That is not a rounding error. That is a broken product.

For teams building analytics pipelines, competitor tracking tools, or any kind of recurring data collection, the official API's cost structure is not a pricing inconvenience. It is a fundamental mismatch with how those systems need to operate.

Scraping APIs solve most of this. No OAuth setup, no app review, no waiting. You get an API key, hit an endpoint, and receive structured JSON. Proxy rotation, anti-bot handling, and pagination are managed on the provider's side. A working pipeline can be running in hours. At scale, the cost per tweet is roughly 10 to 50 times lower than the official API.

The official API still has a real use case: compliance-sensitive, low-volume access where you need the official paper trail. Regulated industries, academic research with institutional requirements, government procurement. Outside of that narrow band, scraping APIs win on nearly every dimension that matters for production use.

The right move is to test against your actual workload before committing to anything. Run your real keywords at your expected volume. An hour of that tells you more than any feature table.
