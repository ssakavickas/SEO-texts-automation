Most Twitter monitoring setups are just polling loops with a better name.

They run on a schedule, pull recent tweets, and hope nothing important slipped through. The moment you need to react in seconds, that approach fails.

Here is what a real-time pipeline actually looks like.

The core problem: polling is reactive at the wrong speed. A competitor announcement or a PR issue gaining traction does not wait for your cron job.

Key takeaways from the full guide:

- Filter Rules run continuously and push matching tweets the moment they are detected, not on a schedule
- You choose between WebSocket for live dashboards and Webhooks for reliable backend delivery with automatic retries
- Managing rules at scale is straightforward, with endpoints to pause, audit, and debug without deleting anything

The architecture is clean: define a rule, connect a consumer, process events as they arrive.
